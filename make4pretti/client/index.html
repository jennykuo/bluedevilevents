Basic User Guide

Creating an Event

Blue Devil Events was designed with the Duke Community in mind. As students, we've recognized the lack of a central source for information about the ongoings on campus. Many resources such as DukeGroups and Duke Calendar only post large-scale or official events. However, we know many students host smaller, open events such as weekend BBQs or off-campus pool parties for Dukiesz. Whether you're a student looking for something to do in between classes, a visitor looking fot explore, or looking for more publicity for your event, Blue Devil Events was created for you. All events are viewable to the public for people to search through. Please do not post any information that you do not want made to the public. Do not post private events. Think of Blue Devil Events as your Yik Yak for campus-related (or student/faculty-hosted) events. We are not a calendar, we're simply a resource for you to publish your open events and search for Duke-related events.

Search Function

We provide several ways for you to find events you're interested in or looking for. If you know details about the event (event host, location, etc.) but may have forgotten about key details such as the time, our search function allows you to easily find the events you're looking for by inputting any information you may know about your event. Alternatively, if you're simply looking for an event to pass the time, you can search for keywords in event names, for search for events with tags of interest.

Our search function is very user-friendly and here to help any user find an event they'd like. If you don't have any strong preferences, simply select the date of your choice to see all events for that particular day.

You can choose to search with as many (or as few) features as you'd like-- whatever helps you find that event!

About Blue Devil Events

Fear of missing out (aka FOMO) on campus is real, and it is only made worse by the lack of an easily accessible, centralized system for students to track what is happening on campus. While Duke's events calendars may provide some base level of exposure to large/academic events, student groups have a hard time promoting their events through digital outlets (outside of Facebook, which - if we're honest - is often too crowded for it's own good). Blue Devil Events hopes to provide a laptop and mobile-friendly resource for students to access on-campus events in one uniform location. Whether you're bored waiting between classes or looking to explore a new interest, Blue Devil Events has you covered.
